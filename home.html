Personas and Storyboarding

An Overview

When you wake up tired in the morning, wouldn't it be nice to have a freshly-brewed cup of coffee ready to go? One coffee maker tries to address exactly that. By pre-setting a brew time and loading the necessary ingredients beforehand, you can have this coffee maker brew your cup at the perfect time. While this may be nice, is the interface well-designed enough to actually use?

The Interface

Let's take a look at some sketches of the coffee maker.

The coffee maker is able to produce coffee in two batch sizes: single-serve and carafe (multiple servings). It's important to note that the machine separates slots for loading in ground coffee and water for both batch sizes.

For the purposes of this project, we'll also be paying particularly close attention to the display (labeled B).

As you can see, the display has multiple buttons, a screen, and a switch. The HOUR and MINUTE buttons allow for adjusting the time and BREW OPTIONS allows users to select "bold" or "reg" brew. The PROGRAM button performs various functions necessary for toggling on the auto-brew feature, and it also has an indicator light to signal that the auto-brew feature is set. The switch allows users to choose a batch size, and the BREW NOW button starts and cancels a brew immediately.

The problem that the interface is trying to solve is how to allow the users to quickly and intuitively preset a brew time and load the necessary ingredients to brew a cup of coffee.

Some Observations

For my observations, I asked a few friends to preset a time to brew a carafe of coffee. To do so, the interface requires them to:

  1. Load water into E (from the diagram above).
  2. Load coffee grounds into F.
  3. Press and hold PROGRAM while simultaneously adjusting the time with the HOUR and MINUTE buttons.
  4. And then press PROGRAM again to turn on the auto-brew, which will turn on the indicator light, meaning they've successfully preset a brew time.

Sophia

  • Having load the ground coffee, she goes to load the water. Instead of loading it in slot E (for carafe), she pours it into slot D (for single-serve).
  • She presses BREW OPTIONS multiple times and soon realizes that it does not function to set the preset time.
  • She presses PROGRAM and nothing happens. She then presses and holds it, which brings up the preset time. She releases it and it goes away.
  • She figures she needs to press and hold PROGRAM while simultaneously adjusting the clock to set the auto-brew.
  • Having set the auto-brew time, she is unsure if it will actually brew, saying "Did that do anything? I don't see anything changing".
  • When the time passes and it does not brew, she presses PROGRAM to once again set the brew time. This, however, turns on the indicator light.
  • Realizing that this must be how auto-brew is set, she readjusts the time as before, and this time makes sure to press PROGRAM again to turn on indicator light.

Ethan

  • Like Sophia, Ethan pours water into the slot for single-serve instead of for carafe.
  • He presses PROGRAM. This does nothing.
  • So, he then cycles through all the other buttons, and could not find where he can set the preset time. He gives up after ~2 minutes and asks for my help. I direct him to hold PROGRAM.
  • After he does this, he notices that the screen time changes, so he attempts to adjust it via the HOUR and MINUTE buttons. As he does not hold PROGRAM while doing this, the preset time doesn't change.
  • He asks for my help again, and I direct him to hold PROGRAM while simultaneously adjusting the time. He then adjusts the preset time.
  • He then presses PROGRAM again, which turns on the indicator light and confirms the preset. Later, he would tell me he did this accidentally.

Jori

  • Unlike Sophia and Ethan, Jori pours the water into the correct place.
  • Before going further, he plays around with the buttons to "know what does what".
  • He presses PROGRAM and it does nothing.
  • He presses BREW OPTIONS repeatedly, and it does nothing helpful for setting brew time.
  • He presses and holds BREW NOW, which only immediately begins brewing instead of setting a time.
  • However, he realizes he can try holding PROGRAM, thus toggling the preset brew time.
  • He adjusts the time while holding PROGRAM, which allows him to change preset time.
  • After this, he is unsure whether the brew will start on its own as he expects it to. However, he notices the indicator light and presses PROGRAM again. This then confirms the auto-brew is set.

The Interviews

After observing the participants, I interviewed them about their experience. Some questions included:

  • Have you had prior experience operating a coffee making machine?
  • Have you used products with similar interfaces? If so, in what applications?
  • Was there any aspect of the interface you found initially confusing? If so, which aspects?
  • On a scale of 1 to 6 (1 = not difficult at all, 6 = very difficult) how would you rate the difficulty of learning how to use the interface to preset the brew time?
  • Did you have difficulties with the task? If so,
    • Please describe the difficulty.
    • How did you resolve it, if you resolved it?
  • On a scale of 1 to 6 (1 = always, 6 = never), how often did an action (ex. pressing HOUR button) lead to an outcome you expected?
  • Did any action(s) lead to an outcome you did not expect? If so,
    • What action(s)?
    • What outcome did you expect?
    • What was the actual outcome?

Sophia

Despite having operated coffee machines before, Sophia says she struggled with this one, particularly when loading water for carafe brewing. She asks rhetorically, "Where the heck was I supposed to put in the water"? Also, while she did manage to preset the brew time, she was unsure if it was actually on or not. She says she did not see the indicator light. Rating the difficulty of the interface, she gives it a 4, meaning somewhat difficult. She attributes this to the straightforwardness of most of the buttons with the exception of PROGRAM. She also says that her actions usually produced outcomes that aligned with what she expected. The notable exception would be, again, the PROGRAM button, which did nothing when she pressed it the first time.

Ethan

Like Sophia, Ethan has operated coffee machines in the past, which have similar interfaces with this one. Ethan notes that he did not find most of the buttons confusing, but he was very confused with where to load in the water. Like Sophia, he also poured water into the wrong location. He says that "the whole set timer thing was new to me", which would explain why he gave the interface a high difficulty rating of 5. Further, he explains that, while most of the interface worked as expected, the PROGRAM button was "weird". He would not have intuitively known to hold onto PROGRAM while simultaneously adjusting time if I had not helped him.

Jori

Unlike Ethan and Sophia, Jori has never operated a coffee machine. Despite this, he was the only participant to pour water into the correct location for carafe brew. He attributes this to having played around with the machine before doing anything. He gives the interface difficulty a rating of 2, indicating it was fairly straightforward for him. When asked about any difficulties, like the other two participants, he says he had a bit of trouble with the PROGRAM button, adding that it's "not labeled as it should be". Instead, he offers that it should be labeled "PRESET BREW". He notes that all actions had the expected outcome, but "PROGRAM gave me a little bit of trouble".

Meet the Personas

Studious Susie

Meet Studious Susie, a 20-year-old junior at Brown. As her name implies, Susie studies hard so that she can excel in her schoolwork. Like many students, Susie best manages her time by carefully planning out her schedule in advance. She notices that every morning she spends a little too much time brewing coffee, and would rather spend it getting ready for classes.

One interface problem that Susie faces is that she can sometimes accidentally forget to preset the brew the night before. This is very inconvenient for her since she likes a tidy schedule. She also sometimes may pour water into the wrong place or set the wrong time, especially if she's super tired from a long day of schoolwork.

Susie embodies the typical user of this interface. In addition to being part of a demographic that is a major consumer of coffee (college students), she is also the type of person who looks for ways to save some time when they can. Like many other users, she values the convenience of having her coffee on demand, even if she has to put in a little work beforehand.

Care-free Cory

Meet Care-free Cory, a 27-year-old civil engineer for a construction firm based in Boston. Having gone through the gruel of college and landed his dream job, he's enjoying the big-city life. You can say he's pretty care-free. After early morning jogs, he brews a pot of coffee while freshening up for work. However, he isn't too happy that by the time he's done cleaning up, the coffee has usually gotten lukewarm.

One interface problem that Cory is that, like Susie, he may sometimes just forget to preset the brew, which could certainly disrupt his schedule. Another problem is that he may forget to actually turn on the preset brew because he neglects to make sure the indicator light is on. When he goes to grab his coffee, he'll be in for a shock to see that his brew never started like he expected. He'll have to weigh these potential costs with the benefits.

Cory embodies the typical user of this interface. Like Susie, he is part of a major demographic that would utilize this interface (busy young adult). Unlike Susie, however, he is not necessarily seeking to improve his morning efficiency (he thinks he's already got that down), but he's looking for ways to keep his brew fresher and hotter for longer. Many users would easily relate to this, since many folks seek to get the most out of their cup of joe. The hotter the better.
